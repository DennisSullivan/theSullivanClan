Wedding Day

Bill & Lois • June 29, 1946

On a warm June day in 1946, just months after returning from service in the Southwest Pacific, Bill Sullivan stood beside Lois in a moment that marked the beginning of a lifetime. Their wedding was simple, heartfelt, and full of the quiet optimism that defined postwar America. These photographs preserve the joy, the hope, and the unmistakable glow of two people stepping into their future together.

About the Day

Bill’s calm, dignified presence — the same steadiness he carried as a navigator aboard B‑24 Liberators — shines through in these images. Lois’s warmth and grace balance him perfectly. Together, they embody the spirit of a generation rebuilding their lives with hope, resilience, and love.

As more restored photographs are added, this gallery will grow into a visual tribute to the day that began the Sullivan family as we know it.
